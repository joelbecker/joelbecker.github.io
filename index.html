Hi. I'm Joel Becker.


I work as a data scientist at Shopify where I build scalable data assets, pipelines, and systems that support an innovative community of decision makers and developers. Before Shopify, I developed open source entity matching and data extraction tools with the University of Waterloo's NetLab, enabling our interdisciplinary research team's leading-edge research in large-scale collaboration networks.

I also hold a Bachelor of Knowledge Integration from the University of Waterloo, where I honed my data science and interdisciplinary research skills. My own research is focused on complex social phenomena, from large-scale trends in the intellectual diversity of social science research teams, to the impact of workplace supervisors' race and gender on their subordinates' reactions to fair or unfair treatment, to online responses to the Syrian refugee crisis. I believe that the frontier of data science is not just defined by technical achievement, but also by data scientists' ability to engage with the social, ethical, and societal context of our work.


Projects

A quick look at some of my work, from open source software development to data science at scale.

Eventstream Pattern Mining

My team of data scients turned contextless data points into meaningful insights using SparkML's powerful frequent pattern mining capabilities. Turning data into knowledge is hard, because real-world events are complex, interconnected, and only meaningful in context. Given a high-volume, high-velocity source of unprocessed event data, we dug deep to find common patterns in the event stream. This foundational work can be scaled to put billions of data points into context, empowering cross-functional stakeholders to understand the needs of their audience. Image: Matthew Henry // Burst

SQL Coach

SQL Coach leverages cutting-edge technologies to deliver real-time feedback to train Shopify employees how to craft elegant and performant SQL queries. SQL best practices are difficult at best for data scientists and beginners alike. SQL Coach takes data education to the user by giving specific and actionable feedback to users after they submit an SQL query. The project leverages Structured Streaming in Apache Spark with Apache Kafka data streams, as well as existing propriety systems, to provide a low-maintenance and high-performance solution. Image: Nicole De Khors // Burst

recordlinkage

recordlinkage is a powerful open-source toolkit for matching and merging data from multiple sources. I dramatically improved performance by identifying inefficiencies in a major performance bottleneck, making several key algorithms up to 97 times faster. I also contributed important new features, designing and implementing an all-new workflow for fusing records and handling data conflicts, as well as collaboratively implementing two new record comparison algorithms . Image: Stella Dauer // Flikr //
