Scanography Study One: Postscript

  • ↓

What I like to call Scanography (more commonly referred to as "Xerox Art") has become somewhat of an obsession of mine. I love getting in the flow of interfering with scans, not knowing exactly what will come out. The more I've done it, the more it's transitioned from a semi-random process to a chaotic collaboration. Zines were commonly made on Xerox copiers, and while I arrange mine digitally, scanning is a major part of my workflow. When I create zines I usually cycle between writing and then using collage, scan art, and glitch art to find visual anchors for the ideas I'm exploring. Computers may offer infinite creative possibilities - but I rarely fall in love with a digital project as easily as I do the happy accidents at the copy machine.

Visions of Entropy is part of Stay with Me, CAFKA’s 2023 biennial exhibition of public art.
